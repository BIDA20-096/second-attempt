About Monaco

Facts About Monaco

    Monaco is the second smallest nation in the world (after Holy See), and the second most densely populated. Located in Europe, it is divided into 10 wards.
    It borders France on three sides and the Mediterranean Sea on its fourth.
    Tourists are attracted to Monaco by its weather and casino, and its glamorous reputation. Ruled by the House of Grimaldi since 1297, the city-state’s sovereignty was declared in 1861 after centuries of being passed between Genoa, France and Sardinia. Mussolini’s troops invaded in 1943, swiftly followed by the Nazis, but the post-war era was happier and more glamorous, encapsulated by Prince Rainier III’s marriage to the actress Grace Kelly. In 2005, their son, Albert II, took the throne.
